# HAZ-007: SWMR deployment misfit

**HDF5 SSP vulnerability tags:** `OPS|LIB`

**Subsystem / component:** SWMR mode, filesystem semantics, writer operation constraints, and reader consistency expectations in deployed environments.

**Hazard family:** H2 Hybrid divergence, H3 Unsafe durability signaling, H8 Operational, privacy, or supply-chain exposure

## Preconditions

- The deployment uses or plans to use SWMR for shared-file access.
- The underlying filesystem or storage environment may not match SWMR assumptions, including POSIX `write()` semantics.
- Writer behavior, allowed operations, or reader expectations are not fully aligned with SWMR constraints.

## Accident chain

### Trigger

SWMR is enabled in an environment or usage pattern that does not satisfy its semantic assumptions.

### Unsafe state

Readers and writers operate under a coordination model that appears safe but does not actually preserve coherent visibility guarantees in that environment.

### Loss / impact

Readers can see stale or inconsistent state, correctness surprises appear in production, and workflows may trust a coordination model that is not valid on the target storage stack.

## Risk assessment

Risk scoring used: **Severity (1-5) x Likelihood (1-5) = Risk (1-25)**

Risk bands: **1-5 Low, 6-10 Moderate, 11-15 High, 16-20 Very High, 21-25 Critical**

### Severity

**4 - Major.** A misfit SWMR deployment can undermine shared-file correctness and produce hard-to-diagnose read or publication failures.

### Likelihood

**3 - Possible.** The hazard is deployment-specific, but storage heterogeneity and partial understanding of SWMR constraints make it realistic.

**Risk:** **12 (High)**

## Detectability

Moderate. Reader errors and stale-read symptoms can expose the issue, but correctness drift may remain hidden without environment-specific integration testing.

## Unsafe control actions

- **Not provided when needed:** The deployment does not verify that the target filesystem and access pattern satisfy SWMR requirements before enabling the mode.
- **Provided when unsafe:** SWMR is used in an environment with incompatible write semantics or unsupported writer behavior.
- **Wrong timing/order:** Readers are allowed to depend on SWMR visibility before the writer has followed the required publication sequence.
- **Too long/too short:** SWMR assumptions are applied beyond the subset of operations the mode safely supports.

## Safety constraints

- SC-1: SWMR shall only be enabled on filesystems and workloads that have been validated against its required semantics.
- SC-2: Writer operations that fall outside supported SWMR constraints shall be rejected or routed to a different publication model.
- SC-3: Deployments shall document the exact reader and writer guarantees they rely on when SWMR is enabled.

## Mitigations

### Prevention

Validate filesystem semantics in staging, follow SWMR programming constraints, and document allowed writer operations and reader expectations for each deployment target.

### Detection

Add SWMR-specific integration tests on supported filesystems and monitor reader errors, stale reads, and retry patterns in production.

### Recovery / containment

Fall back to an externally coordinated publish model when SWMR assumptions fail, isolate suspect shared files, and republish from a verified writer state when needed.

## Tests / evidence

- Crash injection: Interrupt SWMR writers at publication boundaries and verify reader behavior after restart and reopen.
- Malformed-input or fuzz coverage: Exercise reader reopen and validation paths with SWMR-produced files from incompatible or misconfigured environments.
- Concurrency coverage: Run SWMR readers and writers on each supported filesystem and storage class, including negative tests for unsupported semantics.
- Runtime validation / telemetry: Record filesystem type, SWMR enablement, reader retry behavior, and environment-specific consistency anomalies.
