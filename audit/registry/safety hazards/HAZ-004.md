# HAZ-004: Metadata and artifacts leak sensitive information

**HDF5 SSP vulnerability tags:** `PRV|OPS`

**Subsystem / component:** Metadata naming and attribute conventions, logging, tracing, debug dumps, temporary exports, and retained workflow artifacts.

**Hazard family:** H8 Operational, privacy, or supply-chain exposure

## Preconditions

- Sensitive information may appear in dataset names, attributes, provenance-like metadata, logs, dumps, or temporary files.
- Debugging, tracing, export, or retention behavior can emit artifacts outside the intended privacy boundary.
- Users or operators assume these channels are low-risk or are not aware of what information they expose.

## Accident chain

### Trigger

Verbose logging, debug dumps, temporary export files, or overly descriptive metadata conventions emit or retain information that users expected to remain private.

### Unsafe state

Sensitive information is stored, logged, exported, or retained outside the intended boundary, where it becomes available to operators, other tools, or downstream systems.

### Loss / impact

The system creates a privacy breach, increases operational exposure, and can reveal identifiers, workflow details, or provenance that enable further misuse.

## Risk assessment

Risk scoring used: **Severity (1-5) x Likelihood (1-5) = Risk (1-25)**

Risk bands: **1-5 Low, 6-10 Moderate, 11-15 High, 16-20 Very High, 21-25 Critical**

### Severity

**4 - Major.** Information leakage may not break file integrity, but it can expose sensitive data, violate policy, and create lasting downstream risk once artifacts are distributed.

### Likelihood

**3 - Possible.** Logging, tracing, and descriptive metadata are common operational practices, so leakage is realistic unless safe defaults and retention controls are in place.

**Risk:** **12 (High)**

## Detectability

Moderate. Logging reviews, artifact scans, and privacy-focused tests can find many issues, but retained temporary files and downstream copies are easy to overlook.

## Unsafe control actions

- **Not provided when needed:** Sensitive fields are not redacted, filtered, or excluded before metadata, logs, or artifacts are emitted.
- **Provided when unsafe:** Debugging or export features are enabled in environments where their outputs cross privacy boundaries.
- **Wrong timing/order:** Sensitive data is written to logs or artifacts before policy checks, redaction, or retention rules are applied.
- **Too long/too short:** Artifacts are retained too long, or temporary files exist longer than the operational window that justified their creation.

## Safety constraints

- SC-1: The system shall not emit metadata, logs, traces, or artifacts containing sensitive information unless that exposure is explicitly intended and controlled.
- SC-2: Production-safe defaults shall minimize logging, dumps, and temporary artifacts that can cross privacy boundaries.
- SC-3: Retention, cleanup, and redaction rules shall be defined and enforced for all debug and export paths.

## Mitigations

### Prevention

Review metadata conventions, adopt redaction guidance, disable verbose traces by default in production, and constrain where temporary exports and debug artifacts are written.

### Detection

Scan logs and artifacts for sensitive fields, add privacy-focused review checklists, and monitor for unexpected dump creation or retention-policy violations.

### Recovery / containment

Purge exposed artifacts, rotate or restrict access where leakage occurred, and notify affected downstream owners so copied data can be contained or removed.

## Tests / evidence

- Crash injection: Verify that failure-handling and diagnostic paths do not emit additional sensitive dumps or logs during abnormal termination.
- Malformed-input or fuzz coverage: Exercise parser and error-reporting paths with crafted metadata to confirm they do not echo sensitive content unsafely.
- Concurrency coverage: Test concurrent logging, export, and cleanup workflows to ensure temporary artifacts are not left behind or exposed across processes.
- Runtime validation / telemetry: Track dump generation, retention-policy violations, unexpected artifact locations, and redaction failures.
